A painting of ‘Amadeo’

Armand’s long and dark history meant that he was reserved and elusive despite his status as the coven leader. After he welcomed Louis into his coven, the two drew close over time and eventually became lovers. Despite their relationship, there was still a lot that Louis did not know about Armand. In an attempt to resolve this issue, Armand snuck Louis into the Louvre, showing him a painting that had deep ties to his life as a human and finally confiding in him about his past.

The script for the trial against Louis and Claudia

Armand’s relationship with Louis began to distract him from his responsibilities as the coven leader and when the vampires find out that Louis and Claudia attempted to kill their maker (a violation of their rules), Armand is forced to chose between Louis and the coven. Despite his love for Louis, he choses the coven, who stage a public trial to kill Louis and Claudia for their crimes. Louis is mysteriously saved from his execution, and forgives Armand, who he believes is responsible. However, decades later it is revealed that it was in fact Armand that wrote and directed the trial that took Louis’ beloved Claudia from him.
