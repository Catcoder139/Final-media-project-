Illustration of Sekhmet

As a human, Drolta lived in Southern Egypt as the High Priestess of Sekhmet, the Egyptian goddess of war, vengeance and healing. In the year 1199 CE, the temple of Sekhmet was attacked by raiders and vampires, who killed worshippers and stole the body of the goddess. After the attack, Drolta managed to kill one of the vampires and, wishing to become stronger, drank his blood, thus becoming a vampire herself.

In an attempt to bring her goddess back to life, Drolta sired many vampires and fed them the blood of Sekhmet, which would continually result in their deaths. Drolta eventually found a suitable candidate in Erzsebet Báthory, a ruthless noblewoman imprisoned for her countless murders. Erzsebet survived the drinking of Sekhmet’s blood, making her an avatar of the goddess and marking the beginning of Drolta’s loyalty to her.

Ka and Ba statues

After over a century of searching for the remains of Sekhmet, a necessity for reviving the goddess, Drolta was finally successful and began to organise the ritual that would grant Erzsebet Báthory the goddess’ power. Erzsebet already possessed the Ka (blood) of Sekhmet; by consuming the goddess’ heart, and thus her Ba (body), the vampire monarch was granted the power of Sekhmet. Despite this power, Erzsebet was gravely wounded by those revolting against her, causing Drolta to realise that she lacked the true strength needed to embody her goddess. With no further use for her, Drolta drank her former master’s blood, killing her and taking the power of Sekhmet for herself.
