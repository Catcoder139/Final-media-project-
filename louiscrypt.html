An Old Photograph of Storyville

Even after becoming a vampire, Louis had to endure endless racial discrimination from the other businessmen in Storyville. The growing success of his business meant that the attacks against him by his white counterparts became more ruthless and cunning. Things came to a boiling point when Louis’ business was forced to close, leading him to kill the man responsible in a fit of rage and display his body in protest. This led to immediate backlash in the form of violence and fires breaking out across Storyville.

Louis’ sword cane

In 1910 New Orleans, Louis’ identity as both a black man and the owner of a successful business made him the target of both financially and racially motivated threats. Dressing in expensive suits and arming himself with a blade disguised as a cane, he carried himself proudly through the streets of Storyville. It was here that he brandished his word against his own brother for disturbing his business, leading him to be noticed by the vampire Lestat, who had just arrived in New Orleans. This was the start of Lestat’s affection for Louis, who he eventually turned into his immortal companion.

Louis' mask from the Mardi Gras party

After realising the extent of Lestat’s abuse and control, Louis finally agreed to aid Claudia (his adoptive daughter/sister) in her plans to kill their vampiric creator. Claudia convinced Lestat to throw a Mardi Gras party to commemorate their upcoming departure from New Orleans; here, Louis distracted Lestat by accompanying him all night, dancing with him and even kissing him despite their onlooking guests. Later, Lestat was poisoned by Claudia and finally ‘killed’ by Louis.
